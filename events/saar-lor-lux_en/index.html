CODING DA VINCI Saar-Lor-Lux

DE FR EN


Coding da Vinci, the hackathon for open cultural data, brings actors from the worlds of culture and technology together in a collaborative format - to learn from each other and co-create exciting ideas and visions on the basis of open cultural data. In 2020 Coding da Vinci comes to the cross-border region Saar-Lor-Lux (including Saarland / Germany, Grand Est / France, Luxembourg as well as Wallonia / Belgium). For the first time, Coding da Vinci will not only cross the boundaries between culture and technology but move back and forth across national borders to explore and experiment with the fascinating world of open culture.

ONBOARDING-WORKSHOPS:
OPEN AND PREPARE YOUR DATA

"Onboarding-Workshops" allow interested actors from cultural institutions in the greater Saar-Lor-Lux region to better understand how they can create and "open" their cultural data and make it available for use in the hackathon. Three workshops allow participants from across the region to discuss with open data experts what role open cultural data can play in strategies of audience and organizational development and become familiar with common data formats and open licenses.
Registration: per email (cdv@k8.design)

KICK-OFF:
CO-CREATING WITH OPEN DATA

During the two-day kick-off held on May 16-17th, 2020 at Technoport Belval in Esch-sur-Alzette (LUX) participants co-develop ideas, projects, and prototypes in mixed teams (combining the worlds of culture and technology). While a conventional hackathon leaves little time for project development, teams in Coding da Vinci Saar-Lor-Lux can continue to cooperate for seven weeks following the kick-off event.

SPRINT:
COLLABORATIVE PROTOTYPING

During this seven-week sprint teams continue to develop their ideas. Those who wish to come on board or join an existing team after the kick-off can still do so during the sprint phase.

FINAL CEREMONY:
SHOW AND SHARE YOUR WORK

All outcomes - from source code to images or interactive installations (all to be published under an open licence) - will be presented to the public during a final ceremony on July 4th, 2020 in the Moderne Galerie of the “Stiftung saarländischer Kulturbesitz” (Saar Cultural Foundation) in Saarbrücken (DE). A jury of open data enthusiasts assesses these outcomes and chooses the awardees.

INFORMATION FOR PARTICIPANTS

Cultural institutions from across the region make sets of open cultural data available to Coding da Vinci participants. All data sets will be available for download via the Coding da Vinci data portal.

If you come from the world of culture and / or technology and would like to become part of a co-creation process in the context of Coding da Vinci Saar-Lor-Lux 2020, we encourage you to participate, especially if you want to:

  • make a contribution to the growing culture of open cultural data that makes cultural heritage available to a wider public
  • co-create a project in the context of the hackathon that uses open cultural data (including at least one of the data sets provided by the hackathon’s partners)
  • register your project on hackdash
  • present your project during the final ceremony on July 4th, 2020 in Saarbrücken
  • publish your project under an open licence (e.g. Open Source or Open Definition)

Do you have questions or suggestions? Contact us via email or Twitter and we will get back to you!

INFORMATION FOR CULTURAL INSTITUTIONS

Coding da Vinci offers a unique opportunity for data providers - museums, libraries, archives, memorial sites, cinemas, theaters, etc., but also private and research collections interested in the creation and use of open cultural data - to collaborate with other culture and technology enthusiasts and co-create new, exciting forms of access to digital collections.

In case you are not familiar with open cultural data, or do not know how the "opening" of cultural content might support you and your organization, we are organizing three so-called "onboarding workshops" to bring you on board, to provide information on common open data formats and open licenses, but above all, to inspire you to become part of the open culture movement. Where- and whenever possible, we will support you in the creation of a data set for Coding da Vinci.

Collaborating with other participants will allow you to find out more about new audiences, explore new ways of looking at familiar objects, and attract funders for digital cultural heritage initiatives. Make your digital collections available under an open licence for Coding da Vinci Saar-Lor-Lux 2020!

Questions or suggestions? Contact us via email or Twitter and we will be in touch.

The Challenge

Mash it!

Expand your knowledge by connecting different data sets. We have a large number of such data sets. We have the German Digital Library API. We have the Wikipedia API. We have Open Street Map. .... What are you going to do with this material? Mash it!

Examples: Linked Open Data, data analysis, mashup, create context

Move it!

We want a more participatory culture and lower barriers to access and use: Take cultural objects beyond the museum’s boundaries into the spaces of the digital - move it!

Examples: User experience, augmented reality, social media

Discover it!

Our aim is play-based learning and attribution. Cultural knowledges need to become tangible and relevant. Books sit on library shelves, files in archives, images and sculptures in museums. Different objects - different forms of access. How can we connect these objects with each other in ways that increase access to culture for everyone? Discover it!

Examples: City guides, EduGames, research tools, data visualizations

Improve it!

Combine fun and education, present cultural objects in new ways, use digital collections more effectively - fulfil a cultural institution's wish! Some interpret statues and paintings, others create film archives, preserve audio, or collect plants. Everything is connected to everything, but how can we look at what we have in new ways? How can individual collections be enriched by the world’s wisdom? What does the toolkit of a cultural institution need to look like today? Improve it!

Examples: Build scrapers, improve data quality, tagging, geolocation
